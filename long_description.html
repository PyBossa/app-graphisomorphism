The game your are playing solves instances of the Graph Isomorphism Problem (for short, GIP). Two graphs are said to be isomorphic when there is a one-to-one mapping between their sets of points that preserves the links. To be exact, in our game, we are given two isomorphic graphs and we need to find such a mapping.

GIP is a famous mathematical problem with many applications spanning from the design of electronic circuits to the analysis of social networks. Despite more than half-century of research, GIP has unknown computational complexity. In other words, it is unknown how fast is the fastest algorithm for solving GIP. While GIP is widely believed to be easy, so far, we know it to be easy only for special families of graphs.

Here we ask: how do human beings perform when solving GIP? Do human beings find GIP easier on certain graphs? Is it mostly matter of cognition or there is a "human parameter of complexity" reflecting some mathematical parameter of complexity? We collect data from our game, with the purpose to shed light onto this questions and possibly to learn something new about complexity in general.
